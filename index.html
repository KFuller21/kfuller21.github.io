Passing
5:30–5:45 · 15 min
Passing Triangles
Groups of 3. One player at each cone (~10 yards apart). Pass and move to an empty cone.
  • Stand at a cone. Player 1 passes to Player 2.
  • After passing, Player 1 runs to the empty cone.
  • Player 2 now passes to Player 3, then runs to the empty cone.
  • First 7 min: two-touch. Last 8 min: one-touch.
💡 Key ThingAfter you pass — RUN. Don't watch the ball. Move to the empty cone immediately.

```
Corner Kicks + Free Kicks

  • Free kick: 3 defenders as wall, 10 yards out. Kicker shoots over or around. 5 kicks per player.
```

Thursday — Defense & Game Prep
1v1 defending · Block shape · Light body before Saturday · 5:30–7:00 PM
Attacker ·
Defender
1v1 Defense
5:30–5:50 · 20 min
Force Outside — 1v1
Defender stops attacker from reaching goal. DON'T dive in early — be patient, force toward sideline.
  • Cones 12 yards apart, 20 yards long. Attacker at one end, defender 5 yards in front.
  • Defender runs toward attacker, SLOWS DOWN at 2 yards. No diving.
  • Low stance, knees bent, one foot forward. Watch ball — not player's body.
  • Only tackle when attacker touches ball too far forward. Otherwise — delay.
  • 45-second rounds. Swap attacker/defender after each.
💡 Key ThingWatch the ball, not the feet or body — they can fake with body but not where the ball goes.
```
Defensive block ·
Attackers · Arrows = shift
Block Shape
5:50–6:12 · 22 min
Defensive Block — Shift Together
All 7 defenders move as one unit. Like a sliding door — everyone shifts the same direction.
  • Coach holds ball, walks it left/right/forward. Defenders shift with every move.
  • Stay within 5 yards of the person next to you. If there's a gap — "Close the door!"
  • Add 4 attackers passing. Defenders shift with every pass. Don't rush — hold the line.
  • Progress to 7v7. Defenders hold shape until ball comes into the box.
```

```
Dynamic Movement Stations
4 stations set up in parallel lanes. Players rotate through each station to wake up hips, ankles, and change-of-direction muscles.
  • Station 1 — Carioca: Grapevine sideways, hips rotating. 20 yards and back.
  • Station 2 — Lateral shuffle: Low stance, quick feet side-to-side. Stay in athletic position.
  • Station 3 — Backpedal: Run backward with quick choppy steps. Look over shoulder.
  • Station 4 — Sprint buildup: 50% → 70% → 90% speed. 3 reps. Walk back between each.
Outside (possession) ·
Defenders ·
Ball
Possession
5:42–5:58 · 16 min
5v2 Positional Play
Like the rondo but in a bigger area. Outside players must MOVE along the edges — not stand still. Forces checking to the ball.
  • 5 on the outside of a large hexagonal area (~15 yards across). 2 defenders inside.
  • Outside players: 2-touch max. After every pass, SLIDE along the edge to a new spot.
  • Show for the ball — don't hide behind a defender. Open your body to see the field.
  • Lose the ball? You switch in. Play 2-minute rounds.
💡 Key ThingMove BEFORE the ball comes to you. Check toward the ball. Create passing angles.

Red (Zone B) · → Transition direction
Transition
5:58–6:18 · 20 min
Quick Transition 4v4+2
Two zones, two teams of 4. When you win the ball, attack immediately into the other zone. 2 extra players join from the sideline to create a 6v4 overload.
  • 4v4 in Zone A. When Blue wins the ball, they attack Zone B immediately.
  • 2 Blue players waiting on the sideline join to create 6v4 in Zone B.
  • If Red wins it back, they counter-attack into Zone A. 2 Red players join.
  • 3-minute rounds. Emphasis: the first 3 seconds after winning the ball are the most important — GO FAST.
💡 Key ThingWhen you win the ball: head up, find the forward pass FIRST. Don't pass backward unless you have to.
```

```
Cone Slalom → Speed Dribble
Weave through 5 cones with close ball control, then sprint-dribble 20 yards to the box and take a shot.
  • Set up 5 cones in a zigzag pattern, 8 yards apart.
  • Dribble through using the inside and outside of each foot. Keep ball within 1 yard.
  • After the last cone, push the ball forward and sprint-dribble to the 18-yard line.
  • Take a shot on goal. Jog back, go again. 3 rounds each.
Striker ·
Feeder ·
GK ·
Defender (optional)
Finishing
5:42–6:00 · 18 min
Turn & Shoot
Striker receives the ball with back to goal, takes a touch to turn, and shoots. Simulates receiving a pass in the box under pressure.
  • Feeder passes to striker's feet. Striker faces AWAY from goal.
  • Striker takes one touch to control, turns (left or right), then shoots within 2 touches.
  • Progression: Add a passive defender who applies light pressure from behind.
  • 5 shots each, rotate feeder/striker/GK. Both feet — don't favor one side.
💡 Key ThingBefore you receive the ball, glance over your shoulder to see where the defender is — then turn AWAY from them.

Finishing
6:00–6:18 · 18 min
Volley & Header Circuit
3 stations around the goal. Players rotate through each — practicing volleys, headers, and first-time finishes from crosses.
  • Station 1 (Left): Tosser throws ball waist-high. Shooter volleys with laces. Lock ankle.
  • Station 2 (Center): Tosser lobs ball gently. Shooter heads it down toward corner of goal.
  • Station 3 (Right): Tosser serves a low cross. Shooter redirects first-time into the net.
  • 4 shots per station per player. Rotate every 3 minutes. Keep it moving.
💡 Key ThingFor volleys: lock your ankle, point your toe down, and strike through the center of the ball. Don't swing wildly — just meet it clean.
```

```
Team A ·

Defender · → Transfer
Warmup
5:30–5:44 · 14 min
3v1 Box Transfer
Two small grids side by side. 3v1 in each grid. Complete 5 passes, then play the ball across to the other grid. The defender chases.
  • Set up two 10×10 yard grids next to each other. 3v1 in each.
  • Count passes out loud. After 5 passes, play a long pass into the other grid.
  • Defender from the receiving grid presses immediately. Old defender rests.
  • 1-touch only. If defender wins it, the player who lost it becomes the defender.
Blue (defending → counter) ·
Red (attacking → recover) · → Counter direction
Counter-Attack
5:44–6:04 · 20 min
6-Second Counter
When you win the ball, you have 6 seconds to score. If you don't score in 6 seconds, possession resets. Forces fast decisions.
  • 3v2 in the defending zone. Red attacks, Blue defends.
  • When Blue wins the ball, they counter-attack into the attacking zone within 6 seconds.
  • Red must sprint back to defend. Count out loud: "6… 5… 4…"
  • If Blue scores or time runs out, reset. Alternate which team attacks. 2-min rounds.
💡 Key ThingThe first pass after winning the ball decides everything. Look forward FIRST. Play it forward or dribble forward. Don't hesitate.
4 mini pitches · 4v4 per pitch · Winners promote · Losers relegate
Small-Sided
6:04–6:48 · 44 min
4v4 Tournament — King of the Court
4 mini pitches running simultaneously. 4v4 with no goalkeepers on small goals. 5-minute games — winners move up to the "champion pitch," losers move down.
  • Split 16 players into 4 teams of 4. Assign each team to a pitch.
  • Play 5-minute games. No goalkeepers — small cone goals on each end.
  • After each round: winning team moves UP one pitch. Losing team moves DOWN.
  • Track scores. Pitch 1 is the "Champion Court." Bragging rights only!
💡 Key ThingSmall spaces = faster decisions. You can't dribble past everyone in 4v4. Find the pass, move into space, shoot early.
```

```
Outside (pass) ·

Warmup
5:30–5:42 · 12 min
Circle Rondo — 8v2
8 players on a big circle, 2 in the middle. The circle players keep possession while the 2 try to intercept. 2-touch max.
  • 8 players spread evenly on a circle (~15-yard radius). 2 defenders in the middle.
  • Pass around the circle — 2-touch max. Can skip players to change direction.
  • If a defender touches the ball, the passer who lost it switches in.
  • After 3 min, progress to 1-touch only. Emphasize: "open your body before the ball arrives."
Ball-side players ·
Far-side players ·
Defenders · --- Switch pass
Switching
5:42–6:02 · 20 min
Switch the Play — Find the Space
When the defense packs one side of the field, the other side is WIDE OPEN. Practice the long diagonal pass to exploit the space.
  • 6v4. 6 attackers with the ball. 4 defenders compress to the ball-side.
  • Attackers must switch the ball to the far side within 4 passes.
  • The far-side player receives, takes 1 touch forward, and looks to cross or shoot.
  • Progression: defenders can press the far side after the switch — attacker must play quickly.
💡 Key ThingLook at the FAR side of the field before you look at the NEAR side. The space is always where the ball isn't.
GK ·
Defenders ·
Midfielders ·
Pressers
Build-Up
6:02–6:20 · 18 min
Building from the Back
Practice the GK → CB → CM passing sequence to play out of the back under pressure. Don't just boot it — build with purpose.
  • GK starts with the ball. Passes short to a center-back who checks toward the ball.
  • CB looks to find the midfielder. If pressed, CB can go back to GK to reset.
  • CM receives and plays forward — to a winger or striker making a run.
  • Add 2 passive pressers who gradually increase pressure. Walk through first, then go live.
💡 Key ThingCenter-backs: spread apart when the GK has the ball. Give the GK 2 options — left CB and right CB. Don't bunch up.
```
